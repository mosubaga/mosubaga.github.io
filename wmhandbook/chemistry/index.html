Chemical Bonding

Chemical Bonding

Formal Charge

\[FC=V-(N+\frac{B}{2})\]

V is valence electrons, N is nonbonding electrons, and B is bonding electrons.

Chemical Bonding

Coulombic Attraction

\[E\propto \frac{q_1q_2}{r}\]
Chemical Bonding

Bond Order

\[\text{bond order}=\frac{\text{bonding e}^- -\text{antibonding e}^-}{2}\]
Chemical Bonding

Electronegativity Difference

\[\Delta EN=|EN_A-EN_B|\]

Large differences usually mean more ionic character.

Acids and Bases

Acids and Bases

pH and pOH

\[pH=-\log[H_3O^+],\quad pOH=-\log[OH^-],\quad pH+pOH=14.00\]

The 14.00 sum applies to water at 25 C.

Acids and Bases

Water Ion Product

\[K_w=[H_3O^+][OH^-]=1.0\times10^{-14}\text{ at }25^\circ\text{C}\]
Acids and Bases

Acid Dissociation

\[K_a=\frac{[H_3O^+][A^-]}{[HA]}\]
Acids and Bases

Base Dissociation

\[K_b=\frac{[BH^+][OH^-]}{[B]}\]
Acids and Bases

Henderson-Hasselbalch

\[pH=pK_a+\log\left(\frac{[A^-]}{[HA]}\right)\]
Acids and Bases

Titration Equivalence

\[n_{acid}=n_{base}\text{ after coefficient adjustment}\]

Organic Chemistry

Organic Chemistry

Degree of Unsaturation

\[DU=\frac{2C+2+N-H-X}{2}\]

Halogens count like H; oxygen and sulfur do not affect DU.

Organic Chemistry

Combustion Pattern

\[C_xH_yO_z+O_2\rightarrow CO_2+H_2O\]
Organic Chemistry

Alcohol Oxidation

\[1^\circ\text{ alcohol}\rightarrow\text{ aldehyde}\rightarrow\text{ carboxylic acid}\]
Organic Chemistry

Esterification

\[\text{carboxylic acid}+\text{alcohol}\rightleftharpoons\text{ester}+H_2O\]
Organic Chemistry

Markovnikov Addition

\[H-X+\text{alkene}\rightarrow H\text{ adds to carbon with more H atoms}\]

Useful rule for many hydrohalogenation reactions.

Organic Chemistry

Nucleophilic Substitution

\[\text{Nu}^-+R-X\rightarrow R-\text{Nu}+X^-\]

A nucleophile replaces a leaving group.

Environmental Chemistry

\[ppb=\frac{\text{mass solute}}{\text{mass solution}}\times10^9\]
Environmental Chemistry

Henry Law

\[C=k_HP\]

Gas solubility in a liquid is proportional to gas pressure above the liquid.

\[C(t)=C_0e^{-kt}\]
Environmental Chemistry

Beer-Lambert Law

\[A=\epsilon bc\]

Absorbance can be used to estimate concentration in water or air samples.
