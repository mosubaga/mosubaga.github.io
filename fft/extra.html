Extra

This page provides a comprehensive overview of the optional Chapter IV subquests in Final Fantasy Tactics, focusing specifically on the interconnected narratives and recruitment of Beowulf Kadmus, Reis Duelar, and Cloud Strife.

Overview of the Side Stories

The side stories featuring Beowulf, Reis, and Cloud are among the most complex subquests in the game. They are largely sequential, requiring the player to complete specific battles and trigger events in a particular order. These quests are only available in Chapter IV and often require the presence of Mustadio Bunansa on the party roster to initiate.

The Recruitment Chain

  1. Gollund Colliery: Initiation of the quest to find the "holy dragon" and recruitment of Beowulf.
  2. Nelveska Temple: The pivotal battle where Reis is returned to her human form.
  3. Recruiting Cloud: Triggered by events involving a flower girl and a specialized machine in Goug.
  4. Lionel's New Liege Lord: A late-game subquest focusing on Beowulf's resolution after Reis is human and Mullonde Cathedral is completed.

Character Profiles and Mechanics

Beowulf Kadmus

Beowulf is a holy knight who joins the party during the Gollund Colliery subquest while searching for his lost companion, Reis.

  • Job: Templar.
  • Primary Ability: Spellblade.
  • Key Mechanics: His Spellblade abilities, such as "Chicken" (which lowers Bravery) or "Faith," are powerful status-altering magicks.
  • Limitation: Beowulf's Spellblade abilities cannot target himself; for example, he cannot use the "Faith" ability to raise his own Faith stat.
  • Narrative Resolution: His story concludes in the "Lionel's New Liege Lord" subquest, where players can obtain rare Genji equipment and the Masamune.

Reis Duelar

Reis is first encountered in the form of a Holy Dragon. Through the Nelveska Temple subquest, she can be restored to her human form.

  • Job (Human): Dragonkin.
  • Job (Dragon): Holy Dragon.
  • Key Mechanics: Dragon's Charm is a signature ability with a 100% success rate for recruiting monsters from the Dragon and Hydra families. Unlike the Orator's "Entice," this does not require the "Beast Tongue" support skill.
  • Innate Tame: As a Dragonkin, Reis has the "Tame" ability innately, allowing her to recruit monsters in critical HP by attacking them.
  • Stepping Stone: In her Holy Dragon form, she can be used as a "stepping stone," allowing other units to stand on her to reach elevations three height units higher than normal.

Cloud Strife

A traveler from another world, Cloud is brought into Ivalice via a mechanical device found in Goug.

  • Recruitment Requirement: The player must buy a flower from the flower girl in Sal Ghidos (this is a one-time opportunity; declining prevents Cloud's recruitment).
  • Job: Soldier.
  • Primary Ability: Limit.
  • Key Mechanics: Cloud is one of the few male characters who can equip female-only hair adornments (Ribbons).
  • Materia Blade: Cloud cannot use his "Limit" abilities unless he has the Materia Blade equipped.
  • Targeting: Limit abilities utilize "Tile" targeting (targeting a specific map coordinate) rather than "Unit" targeting. If an enemy moves before the charge is complete, the attack will miss.
  • Synergy: The Time Mage support ability "Swiftness" is highly recommended for Cloud, as it halves the charge time for his Limits.
