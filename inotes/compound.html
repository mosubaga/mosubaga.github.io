The Magic of Compounding ❄️

Albert Einstein reportedly called it the "Eighth Wonder of the World"!

What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is when you earn interest on your original money, AND on the interest you've already earned. It's like a snowball rolling down a hill, getting bigger and bigger as it goes!

Why it's Crucial for YOU

Because you're young, you have the most valuable asset in the world: TIME. The longer you let your money compound, the more explosive the growth becomes in the later years.

Starting at 15 instead of 25 can make a difference of hundreds of thousands of dollars by the time you retire!

The Snowball Effect

Year Balance (Starting with $1,000 at 10% interest)
1 $1,100
10 $2,593
20 $6,727
40 $45,259

Notice how it grows much faster the longer you wait!

Real-Life Examples

Example 1: A 22-year-old invests $150 per month for 30 years at a 7% annual return. Total after 30 years: about $182,996.

If they stop contributing and let it grow for 10 more years at 7%, it becomes about $359,980.

If they keep contributing $150 per month for those extra 10 years, the total is about $393,722.

Example 2: A 30-year-old invests $200 per month for 25 years at a 9% annual return. Total after 25 years: about $224,224.

If they stop contributing and let it grow for 10 more years at 9%, it becomes about $530,821.

If they keep contributing $200 per month for those extra 10 years, the total is about $588,357.

Build Your Own Scenario

Try different ages, rates, and monthly contributions to see the impact over time.

Assumes contributions at the end of each month and a steady annual return.

Make It Real

Small, steady contributions are powerful. Even a few dollars a week can turn into serious money over decades.

Focus on consistency first, then increase the amount as your income grows.
