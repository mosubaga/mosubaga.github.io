ETF stands for Exchange-Traded Fund. It's like a pre-packaged basket of investments!

The Easy Guide to ETFs

Instead of buying 500 different stocks one by one, you can buy one share of an ETF that holds all 500 for you. It's instant diversification!

ETFs trade on the stock exchange just like regular stocks, making them super easy to buy and sell.

Why ETFs are Awesome for Teens

They are low-cost, easy to manage, and they allow you to own a wide variety of assets with just a little bit of money. It's the "set it and forget it" way to invest.

ETF vs Single Stock

Feature Single Stock ETF
Risk Higher (Dependent on 1 company) Lower (Spread across many)
Effort High (Need to research company) Low (Professionally bundled)
Growth Potential for massive gains Steady market-average growth

ETF Labels to Check

These quick checks help you compare ETFs fast:

  • Expense Ratio: Lower fees keep more of your money.
  • Index Tracked: Know what the ETF actually follows.
  • Holdings Count: More holdings usually means more diversity.
  • Trading Volume: Higher volume usually means easier buying/selling.
  • Distributions: See how often dividends are paid.
