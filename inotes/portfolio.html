A portfolio is just a fancy word for all your investments grouped together.

Planning Your Strategy

Your strategy depends on your goals and when you need the money. Since you're a teenager, you likely have a "long time horizon," meaning you can afford to take more risks for higher long-term growth.

Using Index Funds

One of the best strategies for beginners (and even pros!) is using Index Funds. These are ETFs or mutual funds that track a specific market index, like the S&P 500. They are low-cost and give you exposure to hundreds of companies at once.

A Simple Starting Strategy

Asset Class Target % Why?
Stock Index Funds 80% For long-term growth and beating inflation.
Bond Index Funds 10% For stability and some interest income.
Cash/Savings 10% For emergencies and quick access.

Note: This is just an example! Your ideal mix might look different depending on your personal goals.

Rebalancing 101

Over time, one part of your portfolio can grow faster than the rest. Rebalancing means nudging it back to your target mix.

  • Check your mix once or twice a year.
  • Sell a little of what grew too big.
  • Buy more of what fell behind.
  • Stay calm and keep your long-term plan.
