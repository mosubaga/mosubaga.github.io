Understanding Mutual Funds

Mutual funds are similar to ETFs because they hold many different investments. However, they are often "actively managed," meaning a professional fund manager is picking which stocks or bonds to buy and sell to try and beat the market.

Advantages & Risks

Advantages Risks
Professional Management Higher Fees (Management Expense Ratio)
Instant Diversification No Guarantee to Beat the Market
Easy to Invest Small Amounts Tax Implications from Frequent Trading

Are they right for you?

Mutual funds can be great if you want a professional to handle the heavy lifting, but watch out for those fees! High fees can eat into your profits over time.

Active vs Index Funds

Active funds try to beat the market by picking winners. Index funds just follow the market and usually cost less.

If two funds look similar, the lower-fee option often wins over the long run.
