A Steady Guide to Bonds

When you buy a bond, you're basically lending money to a company or the government for a set period. In return, they promise to pay you back your original money PLUS interest.

Bonds are generally considered safer than stocks, but they usually offer lower potential returns. They are the "slow and steady" part of a portfolio.

Why Bonds?

Stability! When the stock market is going crazy, bonds often stay relatively calm. They provide a predictable income stream through interest payments.

Bond Basics

Type Who are you lending to?
Government Bonds Uncle Sam (The Federal Government). Very safe!
Corporate Bonds Big companies. A bit riskier than government bonds.
Municipal Bonds Your city or state. Often used for schools or roads.

Key Bond Terms

These words show up everywhere in bond investing:

  • Coupon: The interest rate the bond pays.
  • Maturity: When you get your original money back.
  • Yield: The actual return based on price and coupon.
  • Credit Rating: How safe the issuer is.
  • Price Risk: Bond prices drop when rates rise.
