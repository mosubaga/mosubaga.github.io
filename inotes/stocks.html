Buying a stock means you own a tiny piece of a real company. Cool, right?

The Friendly Guide to Stock Ownership

Imagine you and your friends start a lemonade stand. If you sell "shares" of your stand to your neighbors, they now own a part of it. If the stand does well, their shares become more valuable.

That's exactly how the stock market works, but with giant companies like Apple, Disney, or Tesla!

Why Buy Stocks?

Stocks generally have the highest potential for growth over a long time. They are a great way to build wealth, but remember: they can be a bit of a roller coaster ride in the short term!

Key Terms to Know

Term What it means
Share A single unit of ownership in a company.
Dividend A portion of a company's profit paid out to stockholders.
Ticker Symbol The short code for a stock (like AAPL for Apple).

Stock Research Basics

Before you buy, make sure you can explain these in your own words:

  • What does the company sell, and who buys it?
  • How does it actually make money?
  • Is the business growing over time?
  • What makes it different from competitors?
  • What big risks could hurt it?
