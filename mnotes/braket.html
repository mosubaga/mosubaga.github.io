Dirac Notation in Pastel Space

Vectors, Inner Products, and Outer Products

Dirac notation packages linear algebra into a compact language: kets for vectors, bras for dual vectors, and brackets for inner products and projections.

1) Kets, Bras, and Brackets

A state vector is written as a ket $|\psi\rangle$. Its conjugate transpose is a bra $\langle\psi|$. Together they form a bra-ket, or inner product.

$$\langle\phi|\psi\rangle = \sum_i \phi_i^* \psi_i$$
Ket $|\psi\rangle$ A column vector representing a state in a vector space.
Bra $\langle\psi|$ The conjugate transpose of the ket, acting as a linear functional.
Inner product $\langle\phi|\psi\rangle$ measures overlap, length, and angle between states.
Orthonormal basis $\langle i|j\rangle = \delta_{ij}$ keeps coordinates clean and projections simple.

2) Outer Product and Projectors

The outer product $|\psi\rangle\langle\phi|$ builds a matrix (operator) from two vectors. It acts on a ket by projecting onto $\langle\phi|$ and re-scaling by $|\psi\rangle$.

$$|\psi\rangle\langle\phi| \, |\chi\rangle = |\psi\rangle \langle\phi|\chi\rangle$$

Projector

For a normalized state $|\psi\rangle$, the operator $|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|$ projects any vector onto the line spanned by $|\psi\rangle$.

Completeness

In an orthonormal basis, the identity is a sum of projectors: $$\sum_i |i\rangle\langle i| = I.$$

Matrix view

With vectors in $\mathbb{C}^n$, $|\psi\rangle\langle\phi|$ is the rank-one matrix formed by $\psi \phi^\ast$.

3) Quantum Mechanics Applications

Dirac notation is the native language of quantum mechanics. States are kets, measurements are operators, and probabilities come from inner products.

Measurement probabilities

The probability of measuring outcome $|i\rangle$ in state $|\psi\rangle$ is $|\langle i|\psi\rangle|^2$.

Observables
