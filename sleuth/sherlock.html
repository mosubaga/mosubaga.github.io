Sherlock Holmes

The world's only consulting detective, known for his proficiency with observation, deduction, and forensic science.

The Detective

Sherlock Holmes is a brilliant but eccentric detective who operates out of 221B Baker Street in London. He is legendary for his ability to solve cases that baffle Scotland Yard, using his razor-sharp logic and keen eye for detail. Holmes's greatness lies in his 'science of deduction,' which allows him to draw complex conclusions from the smallest of clues.

The Author

The Sherlock Holmes series was written by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, a British writer and physician. Doyle first introduced Holmes in 'A Study in Scarlet' in 1887, and the character eventually appeared in four novels and 56 short stories.

Characters

  • Sherlock Holmes - The brilliant consulting detective.
  • Dr. John Watson - Holmes's loyal friend and biographer.
  • Professor Moriarty - Holmes's brilliant and dangerous archenemy.
  • Irene Adler - The only woman who ever bested Holmes.
  • Mrs. Hudson - The long-suffering landlady of 221B Baker Street.
