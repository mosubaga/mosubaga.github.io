Organization

Make your vault easy to add to, easy to search, and easy to trust.

The best beginner setup is not complicated. Use folders for broad places, links for relationships, tags for loose themes, and properties for structured facts you may want to filter later.

The Four Organization Tools

Each tool has a different job. Do not make one tool do everything.

ToolUse it forGood exampleAvoid
FoldersBroad location and ownership.Projects, Areas, Resources, ArchiveDeep nesting where notes disappear.
TagsLoose themes, status, and temporary grouping.#draft, #meeting, #exam-reviewTagging every noun in every note.
LinksRelationships between ideas.[[Bayes theorem]] linked from statistics, AI, and decision-making notes.Orphan notes that never point anywhere.
PropertiesStructured fields for sorting and filtering.status, course, date, owner, sourceAdding fields you never search or filter.

A Simple Starter System

This structure works because it separates fast capture from long-term reference.

Obsidian Vault/ 00 Inbox/ quick notes before sorting
10 Projects/ active work with outcomes
20 Areas/ ongoing responsibilities
30 Resources/ reusable references and topic notes
40 People/ classmates, teammates, stakeholders
90 Archive/ inactive material you may need later

Weekly cleanup rhythm

  • Empty your Inbox into the right project, area, or resource folder.
  • Add two or three links to notes that should know about each other.
  • Rename vague notes like Lecture 5 into useful names like Newton's second law examples.
  • Archive finished projects instead of deleting them.

For Work: Meeting Notes and Plans

For work notes, make the note useful to your future self. A meeting note should show what happened, what changed, and what someone must do next.

Meeting note

Use sections for agenda, decisions, action items, open questions, and linked people or projects.

Business plan

Break big plans into linked notes: customer, problem, solution, risks, budget, timeline, and assumptions.

Project hub

Create one project page that links to research, meetings, decisions, deliverables, and next actions.

Rule of thumb

If a note answers a question, name it as the answer. If a note is a place to collect related notes, name it as a topic hub. Example: Limits and continuity can be a hub; Why epsilon-delta definitions matter is an idea note.
