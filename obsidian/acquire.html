Capture and process

Acquire information without turning your vault into a junk drawer.

Capture is only step one. The useful move is to add context: why you saved it, what it connects to, and what you may do with it later.

Common Capture Paths

Use the lightest method that preserves enough context.

Daily notes

Fastest place for thoughts, tasks, class notes, and meeting notes when you do not know where they belong yet.

Web Clipper

Save web pages, highlights, and page data directly to your vault using Obsidian's free browser extension.

PDFs and files

Attach readings, screenshots, diagrams, and slides, then link notes to the exact source they came from.

Meetings

Capture agenda, decisions, owners, dates, and links to projects or people while the context is fresh.

The Capture Triage

Use this three-question test every time you save something.

Why did I save this?

Add one sentence. If you cannot explain why it matters, archive or delete it.

Where will I need it?

Link it to a project, course, topic, person, or future deliverable.

What is the next action?

Summarize, cite, practice, reply, decide, or convert it into a permanent reference note.

Do not confuse collecting with learning

A vault full of clipped articles can feel productive but still be hard to use. Your own summary, links, and questions are the parts that make captured information useful.
