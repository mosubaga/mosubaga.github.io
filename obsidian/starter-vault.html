Why This Layout Works

It gives every note a temporary place and a long-term place. You can capture quickly during class or meetings, then organize later when you have more context.

  • Inbox keeps unfinished thoughts from blocking you.
  • Projects keeps outcome-based work visible.
  • Areas holds responsibilities that do not really end.
  • Resources stores reusable knowledge by topic.
  • Archive gets old material out of the way without deleting it.

What Goes Where

When in doubt, put it in Inbox first. You can organize during review.

Projects

Anything with a finish line: an exam, paper, job search, app, business plan, trip, or client deliverable.

Areas

Ongoing responsibilities: school, work, finance, health, club leadership, research lab, or personal learning.

Resources

Reference material you want to reuse: concepts, book notes, tutorials, formulas, snippets, frameworks, and checklists.

People

Notes about classmates, coworkers, professors, mentors, stakeholders, and recurring meetings.

Templates

Reusable note shapes for meetings, sources, concepts, projects, weekly reviews, and study questions.

Archive

Finished or inactive material. Archive beats delete when you might need the context later.
